Things you can do!

  • Use extra water from cooking to water plants if at all plants are there in one’s house.
  • Use pet water to water plants.
  • Switch off the tap while washing your hands or brushing your teeth.
  • Collect the water running from your tap when waiting for the water to get hot.
  • Iron clothing that is not dirty but not fresh instead of washing it.
  • Take a shower instead of a bath.
  • Shave with a bowl of water instead of the tap.
  • Save shower water and use it to flush your toilet.
  • Grow drought-resistant plants instead of normal ones.
  • Use a water gardening can instead of a hose.
High water consumption on a short term basis can decrease long term water security.
The usage and wastage of water does not solely reduce water supplies but also fossil fuels, additional resources, and manpower for filtration and processing.
The lack of sufficient potable water can lead to disease and illness in underdeveloped rural areas that lack access to adequate freshwater distribution.
Excessive water wastage can pose a potential threat to aquatic and land species that rely on the water bodies that are being drained for human consumption.
Water wastage takes a toll on the financial resources of households that create it.
Water wastage eventually leads to soil degradation, harming our produce and our crops, and therefore our overall health as a planet.
